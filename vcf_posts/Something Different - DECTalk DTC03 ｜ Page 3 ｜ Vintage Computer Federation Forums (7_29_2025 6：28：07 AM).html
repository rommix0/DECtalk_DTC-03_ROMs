I recently (March) obtained one of the "70-22666-01 DTC03 DECTALK III 8-SLOT BACKPLANE" rackmount modules along with 6 50-16704-01 C5005 DTC-03 cards inside, and had two cards from previously, so I was able to completely fill the rack with all 8 cards.
One piece of the rack is missing, the metal panel (with 16 rectangular holes in it, 2 lining up to each card) which goes in between the hinged magnetic door on the front and the front of the 8 cards. Perhaps someone has an extra lying around.

The problem is the rack's power supply (70-23328-01, also called 30-31096-01 or DECTALK III POWER SUPPLY on some part sites), which makes a rather nasty arcing noise when powered on (with no load); it does seem to provide the correct voltages when measuring the pins with a meter.
The power supply internally is just a PowerMate EVD-165-3001 caged bench supply mounted in a heavy DEC case. I'm suspecting bad capacitors at this point, since even though the caps are all Nippon Chemicon (old name for Nichicon), they are from 1985. Will investigate further.

Based on the backplane (and not realizing Lou had done this already until halfway through) I traced out what all of the backplane pins connect to.
https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1iGxP9pOzrhxkUgDEMFa80xnHCe0CeswMZqSa1H26zqI/edit?usp=sharing

Interestingly, one of the 6 cards which came in the backplane is clearly a prototype! It lacks the 23-xxx markings on the roms (though their contents match the earlier 23-114,115,116,117E6 code roms, along with the 23-028,029,032,033E6 dictionary roms, except for one single rom which may be going bad and has 6 bits flipped), and has a serial number of "DMT003" (DEC manufacturing test, maybe?). The pinout on that prototype card is slightly different from the later ones! It lacks the second 2 pairs of +5v pins and the second 2 pairs of GND pins, but otherwise matches. The TMS32010 DSP code PROMS also match 23-229,230F4 on the final cards.

The backplane itself I have has the lower (used by the prototype) 5v and GND bus pins reinforced with an extra metal bus bar soldered behind each one in addition to the trace on the pcb itself, so perhaps this cage once held more prototype cards and required this as a retrofit due to excess current on just those two pins per rail per card.

Jonathan Gevaryahu AKA Lord Nightmare

P.S. does anyone know what version numbers the different rom sets correspond to? It should be possible through some <ESC>+something commands to make the dtc-03 speak its version number or return it in hex. (Without a working power supply I can't do that here right now)

The only three romsets I know of so far for the DTC-03 are:
(23-028,029,032,033E6 - dictionary, common to all dtc-03 versions I know of; internal datecode is 30 Oct 85)
(23-229,230F4 - TMS32010 DSP code, common to all dtc-03 versions I know of)
23-114,115,116,117E6 - oldest version I know of; internal datecode is 03 Dec 85
23-305,306,307,308E6 - 'middle' version; internal datecode is 06 Apr 87
23-425,426,427,428E6 - 'final' version; internal datecode is 03 Jan 89

Are there any other versions anyone has in their DTC-03 cards?

By overwhelming popular demand, here are the pinouts:

Guide photo to show how I chose to label the pins : http://www.vintage-computer.com/vcforum/album.php?albumid=180&attachmentid=11500

Connector pinout tables : http://www.vintage-computer.com/vcforum/album.php?albumid=180&attachmentid=11501


Yes, the DTC03 certainly has phone line provisions. I did not bother to bring them out to the back of my enclosure since I will not be using the phone interface. However, the pins are indicated in the pinout table.

A few other comments.

1. The +5V lines go through a 3A fuse on the board
2. The +12V lines go through a 1A fuse
3. The -12V lines go through a 3/4A fuse
(this is useful for determining what size power supply to provide)

4. The DB25 pins I mention would be for wiring to a DB25M DTE connector


Hope this helps,
Lou
